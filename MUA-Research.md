# MUA & Wedding MUA Profile/Taxonomy Research (Global + Philippines) — For a Wedding Vendor Site

## TL;DR
- Across all four segments, clients consistently want the same core facts up front — transparent pricing/package inclusions, a portfolio showing their own skin tone, trial policy, longevity, hygiene, travel, team size, and backup/cancellation policy — but the Philippine wedding market adds decisive variables: per-face "entourage" pricing (₱1,500–₱5,000/head), pre-dawn "early call" fees, "HMUA" (hair-and-makeup combined), prenup-shoot makeup, morena-skin/humidity longevity, and GCash/bank-transfer deposits.
- What MUAs want clients to know is largely a mirror image: give complete quoting info up front (date, venue, call time, number of heads/pax, pegs, skin/allergy details), respect that bridal pricing reflects a full-day commitment (kit cost, one-booking-per-day exclusivity, emotional labor), book trials, don't "hide the W-word" or ghost, and judge by portfolio over price — with a specifically Filipino tension around "tawad" (haggling) and undisclosed-wedding bookings.
- For the site: build profile fields around price transparency (starting rate + per-head entourage rate), coverage areas, HMUA/technique tags, trial policy, team size, payment methods, and skin-tone/humidity competence; use taxonomy that matches real Philippine search language (HMUA, bridal package, entourage/pax, prenup, airbrush/HD, morena-friendly, glam, Korean/dewy/no-makeup); and beat local competitors (Kasal, Bridestory, eKasal, Kwyzer) — who mostly hide prices behind "Price Upon Request" — on structured price + verified reviews.

## Key Findings

**1. The universal hiring checklist is remarkably stable.** International guides (The Knot, WeddingWire, Zola, plus dozens of MUA blogs) converge on the same ~12–24 questions: pricing breakdown and what's included; deposit/contract/cancellation; portfolio + experience with the client's skin tone/type; trial availability and cost; products/brands and allergy/sensitivity accommodation; longevity/technique (airbrush vs. traditional/HD); travel/on-location; timing per face and bridal-party capacity; touch-ups/staying through the event; backup artist if the MUA is sick; sanitation; and reviews/social proof.

**2. Wedding context sharpens every criterion.** Bridal work is judged on all-day, photograph-proof longevity, HD/photo behavior, larger-group logistics with early call times, second looks (ceremony → reception), and emotional/day-of reliability. Trials become near-essential; booking lead times stretch to 8–12 months for peak dates; and cancellation/backup clauses matter more because the date is irreplaceable. Wedding beauty is now near-standard: per The Knot Real Weddings Study, 72% of marriers hired a wedding beauty professional.

**3. The Philippine market adds a distinct layer.** "HMUA" (hair & makeup artist) is the default local term — most artists do both. Pricing is bride-rate + **per-face entourage** (₱1,500–₱5,000/head), which is the true swing factor in the bill; add early-call/call-time fees (3–5 AM starts for morning church weddings), out-of-town (OOT)/transportation fees, prenup-shoot makeup, trial/retouch upgrades ("UR"/unli-retouch), and second-look changes. Skin competence means **morena/Filipina undertones** and **tropical-humidity longevity**; payments run through **GCash, bank transfer, and cash**; and social proof lives on Facebook/Instagram plus bridal forums (r/WeddingsPhilippines, blogs) rather than formal review platforms.

**4. From the artist's side, the biggest asks are complete information and respect for the model.** MUAs need date, venue/location, call time, number of heads/pax, desired look with pegs, skin type/concerns, and allergies to quote accurately. They want clients to understand why bridal costs more (kit is a six-figure-peso investment; one booking per day; 2–2.5 hrs per bridal face; long-wear technique; emotional labor), to book trials, prep skin, be punctual, provide food for the team, and not add heads last-minute or haggle. In the Philippines specifically, artists are frustrated by clients who hide that a booking is for a wedding to dodge the "wedding premium," and by "tawad" culture colliding with professional pricing.

**5. Platform gap = opportunity.** Western platforms (WeddingWire/The Knot/Zola) increasingly expose price signals, rich filters, and verified-booking star reviews. Philippine platforms mostly **hide prices** ("Price Upon Request" on Kasal; $/$$/$$$ tiers on Bridestory; negotiable ranges on Kwyzer) and have weak/unused review systems. A site that shows a real starting rate + per-head entourage rate, structured technique/coverage tags, and trustworthy reviews would differentiate sharply.

## Details

### PART 1 — What prospective clients want to know BEFORE HIRING

#### Segment 1.1 — An MUA in general
Clients evaluating any makeup artist search for and ask about:
- **Price & structure:** total cost with a line-item breakdown (service rate, travel, add-ons); deposit amount (industry norm 25–50%, often non-refundable); accepted payment methods; balance deadline. Zola's guidance notes trials, travel, upgrades, and tips change the final total.
- **Portfolio & range:** 12–20 best looks; consistency; variety across skin tones, ages, and styles; real (un-retouched) work, not just filtered studio shots.
- **Style match / niche:** natural vs. soft glam vs. full glam vs. editorial; artists have signatures, and asking one to do the opposite is "a recipe for disaster."
- **Products/brands & technique:** what's in the kit and why; airbrush vs. traditional; whether products suit the client's skin type; longevity claims.
- **Skin tone/type competence:** ability to show portfolio work on the client's complexion and texture; "repeated hedging is a signal."
- **Allergies/sensitivities & hygiene:** patch tests, disposable applicators, brush sanitation (70% isopropyl, no double-dipping), non-expired products; a pro should proactively ask about allergies.
- **Experience & credentials:** years in business; training; licensing/insurance where relevant (in most US states paid work requires a cosmetology/esthetics license; UK equivalents include BABTAC/VTCT).
- **Reviews/social proof:** Google, Instagram, testimonials; watching for patterns in negative reviews.
- **Logistics & timing:** studio vs. mobile; time per face (typical pro application 45–75 min; adds up with talking, phones); response speed (the fastest professional reply often wins).

#### Segment 1.2 — A wedding MUA specifically
Everything above, plus wedding-specific concerns:
- **Bridal specialization & all-day performance:** long-lasting, photograph-friendly formulas; behavior under different lighting and HD cameras (pro cameras strip ~25–50% of makeup saturation, so bridal is applied heavier than clients expect).
- **Package inclusions:** trial, lashes (guides say lashes should always be included), touch-up kit, travel, team fees, and whether the artist stays for touch-ups. "Get everything in writing."
- **Bridal party / group:** group pricing; largest party the artist can accommodate; whether they bring a second artist/assistant to halve the time (makeup ~35–60 min/face → an 8–12-person party = very early start).
- **Trial:** timing (guides vary: The Knot recommends ~3–6 months out; Bespoke Bride argues 3–4 months, not 6–8 weeks; trials often cost ~60–75% of the wedding-day rate); what to bring (inspo, veil, lip color, clean moisturized skin).
- **Backup plan:** what happens if the artist is sick/unavailable — a reliable replacement clause; ideally one wedding booking per day.
- **Skin prep guidance:** pre-wedding skincare timeline; avoiding new actives/facials/peels close to the day.
- **Timing/arrival:** exact arrival and start times keyed to ceremony and photo schedule; itinerary coordination with the planner.
- **Early-morning/travel/holiday fees:** disclosed as legitimate.
- **Insurance/licensing** where venues require it.
- **Cost context & lead time:** Zola's 2026 Wedding Cost Index puts combined wedding hair and makeup at an average of **$982 nationwide (typical range $800–$1,200)**, with **bridal makeup alone right around $305 ($218–$424)**; The Knot's Real Weddings Study cites an average of about **$300 total ($150 hair + $150 makeup)**. Peak US wedding months are October and June (16% of weddings each), so top artists book many months out.

#### Segment 1.3 — An MUA in the Philippines (general, not only weddings)
- **"HMUA" is the default:** clients look for hair-and-makeup combined; grooming for men (~₱800–₱1,500/head) is a distinct, cheaper service.
- **Price ranges are wide and often negotiable:** directory data shows Metro Manila makeup rates spanning roughly ₱500–₱70,000; event/party makeup often ₱1,500 (traditional) to ₱2,000 (airbrush) per head, with group discounts.
- **Morena/Filipina skin competence:** matching warm/olive undertones to avoid an ashy "gray" or "ghost-face" cast; celebrity MUAs (e.g., Rodolfo Sitchon, Jigs Mayuga) publicly emphasize undertone-correct base for morena skin. This is a real, searched-for concern ("morena-friendly").
- **Humidity/longevity:** Philippine makeup formulas and techniques are built for "sauna-like" heat; setting sprays, layering discipline, and long-wear are expected.
- **Payment & booking:** GCash, bank transfer, cash; reservation/downpayment to hold a slot; heavy reliance on Facebook/Instagram pages for portfolio and inquiry (Messenger/Viber/WhatsApp contact).
- **Certifications/training:** HD makeup courses and named academies (e.g., HD Makeup Studio, MUFE Academy) carry weight; "Licensed Professional Makeup Artist" is used as a credential signal.

#### Segment 1.4 — A wedding MUA in the Philippines (the most specific — and highest-value — segment)
This combines wedding + Philippine layers and is where local nuance concentrates:
- **Bride rate vs. per-face entourage — the real bill driver.** Bride-only 2026 Metro Manila reference tiers (per Nuptl): upcoming/budget ~₱4,000–₱10,000; established ~₱12,000–₱30,000 (most couples land here, trial + long-wear included); premium/sought-after ~₱30,000–₱60,000; celebrity/top-name ₱70,000+. **Entourage is charged per face at ₱1,500–₱5,000**, so 8–10 faces can match or exceed the bridal rate. Nuptials.ph cites HMUA packages ₱7,500–₱30,000 and recommends allocating 10–15% of the wedding budget.
- **Entourage/pax packages:** bridal package (bride only), entourage package (up to ~5 additional heads), and groom/grooming; per-head add-ons above the package; flower-girl and "additional head" line items. Filipino entourages are large (principal/secondary sponsors, ninang/ninong, bridesmaids, mothers), so group rates and per-head pricing are central.
- **Retouch tiers:** no retouch (artist leaves after prep), with retouch (one retouch before reception), and **"unli-retouch"/UR** (artist stays 3–5 hours) — a distinctly Filipino menu item, often an upgrade.
- **Prenup-shoot makeup:** a separate, commonly bundled service (with change-looks and unlimited retouch windows). Some brides use the trial as prenup makeup.
- **Early call time:** 3–5 AM starts for 10 AM morning church weddings; a documented "early-call/call-time fee" (e.g., +25% for ceremonies before noon) because the whole entourage must be finished before church. (Context: per PSA 2023 data, ~31% of Philippine marriages are Roman Catholic church rites and ~43% civil — the early-call pressure is most acute for the morning church segment.)
- **Out-of-town (OOT)/travel fees:** explicit per-destination pricing (Tagaytay/Cavite, Batangas, Baguio, then Cebu/Davao/Boracay/Palawan with airfare + lodging shouldered by the couple). Destination/provincial coverage is a real filter.
- **Humidity + morena longevity together:** tear-proof, sweat-proof, 8-hours-to-midnight wear is explicitly marketed and reviewed ("had my makeup done at 8:30 AM, lasted to midnight with minor retouches").
- **Second look:** softer ceremony face → glammed reception, or hair change; raises price because the artist or a standby stays longer.
- **Booking lead time & peak season:** By PSA registered-marriage data (2023), the highest-volume months are **February (12.7%), December (10.6%), and June (10.5%)**; the "-ber" months are also popular. Guides urge booking suppliers 8–18 months ahead for peak/"11.11"/"12.12" dates.
- **Payments:** GCash, bank transfer, cash; downpayment/reservation to lock the date; contracts increasingly used by professional HMUAs.
- **Social proof:** Facebook/IG pages, bridal blogs, and Reddit's r/WeddingsPhilippines; coordinator/photographer referrals are powerful; "featured" and celebrity clients boost tiering.

### PART 2 — What MUAs want prospective clients to know

#### Segment 2.1 — MUAs in general
- **Give complete info to quote:** occasion, date, location (you-travel-to-them or vice versa), number of faces, timing, desired look with inspo, skin type/concerns, and **allergies** (a safety and legal necessity). "We're not mind-readers" — "natural glam" means different things to different people.
- **Come with a clean, bare face** and prepped skin; wearing makeup wastes removal time.
- **Makeup takes longer than you think** (not 20–30 min; phones add ~10 min/client); quality can't be rushed.
- **Trust the kit:** the artist's product/brand choices are deliberate (e.g., avoiding SPF that flashes back in photos); tell them allergies before, not after.
- **Trials are for experimentation; paid event days are for reliable delivery.** Feedback is welcome — speak up during the trial.
- **Deposits/contracts protect both sides**; a signed contract + paid deposit is what actually holds a date; response speed matters.
- **Photography changes makeup:** looks read lighter on camera, so application is heavier than daily wear.

#### Segment 2.2 — Wedding MUAs specifically
- **Book early and do the trial early** (peak months fill a year out; late trials leave no room to adjust). A no-contract booking risks a no-show; a contract holds the artist accountable to appear or send a replacement.
- **You're paying for a whole-day commitment, not one look:** ceremony + shoots + reception, staying on hand, tear-proof longevity, and "peace of mind." The professional fee reflects opportunity cost (no other clients that day) plus team food/transport.
- **Bring pegs but hold them loosely:** the artist adapts the reference to your features; you won't look identical to the peg.
- **Disclose insecurities, skin conditions, and the real timeline;** avoid facials/peels/actives close to the day; sleep and hydrate.
- **Respect the schedule and party capacity:** realistic per-face time caps how many people can be done; last-minute additions need pay/time.
- **Feedback etiquette:** raise concerns privately/directly (many contracts ask dissatisfied brides to message rather than post first).

#### Segment 2.3 — MUAs in the Philippines
- **Pricing has "a method to the madness":** kit is a six-figure-peso investment (multiple high-end foundation shades, RIKI mirrors, etc.); rates also reflect skill with morena undertones and humidity longevity, not just "the same products."
- **"Budget?" inquiries and the W-word:** artists know brides sometimes hide that a booking is for a wedding, or use a "trial"/"regular glam" session for a prenup, to dodge the wedding premium. Responses range from requiring makeup be wiped after a test / doing only half-face trials, to (better practice) insisting on **clear inclusions and mutual respect** rather than "unspoken rules" (per HMUAs Aldrin Jalandoni and Chuchie Ledesma).
- **Tawad/haggling tension:** haggling is culturally normal in Philippine retail, but professional HMUAs push back on it for skilled service; transparency and written inclusions are the antidote.
- **Trials/pegs and skin prep** guidance mirrors global advice, with added emphasis on managing family/"tita" opinions on the day.
- **Payment & booking etiquette:** downpayment via GCash/bank transfer to reserve; one booking per day for weddings.

#### Segment 2.4 — Wedding MUAs in the Philippines
- **Quote drivers clients under-appreciate:** per-face entourage count, early call time (3–5 AM), OOT travel + lodging, second looks, and unli-retouch standby all cost real time/money and must be stated up front.
- **Bridal takes ~2–2.5 hrs vs. ~1.5 hrs for regular glam;** wedding-day interruptions (coordinator, photographer) are factored in.
- **The "natural" look is the hardest** to execute and make last a humid all-day event — that's the expertise being paid for.
- **Don't deceive to save money;** artists may decline future bookings from clients who hide a wedding. Clarity, transparency, and mutual respect are the ideal.
- **Team/assistants for big entourages:** finishing a large party on time needs multiple artists; slot order (entourage first, bride last) is planned at the trial.

### PART 3 — Synthesis for the vendor site

#### 3A. Recommended MUA profile fields/sections
Ordered by importance; ★ = especially decisive in the Philippine market.

**Identity & credibility**
- Business/artist name; solo artist vs. team/studio ★ (clients want to know who actually shows up)
- Years of experience / year established
- Training/certifications (e.g., HD makeup course, academy) and "Licensed Professional Makeup Artist" flag
- Short bio + signature style statement

**Pricing & packages ★ (biggest differentiator locally)**
- **Starting bridal rate** (show a real number, not "Price Upon Request")
- **Per-head / per-face entourage rate** ★ and entourage package tiers (up to 5 heads, 10+, etc.)
- Groom/grooming rate
- Trial fee (and whether deducted from balance / included)
- Prenup-shoot makeup rate ★
- Retouch options: no-retouch / with-retouch / **unli-retouch (UR)** ★
- Second-look add-on ★
- Early-call/call-time fee ★
- Out-of-town/travel fee schedule by area ★
- What's included (lashes, skin prep, touch-up kit, standby hours)

**Techniques & looks**
- Technique tags: airbrush, traditional, HD
- Finish/look tags: natural, no-makeup makeup, dewy/"flawless," glam/full glam, soft glam, Korean look, morena-friendly ★, editorial
- Hair services offered (updo, veil placement) — confirm "HMUA = both"

**Skin & safety**
- Skin-tone/type competence, esp. **morena/Filipina undertones** ★ and oily/humid-climate longevity ★
- Allergy/sensitivity accommodation; hygiene/sanitation statement
- Products/brands used

**Logistics & policies**
- Coverage areas / willingness to travel to provinces / destination ★
- Team size & max faces per hour; how many artists for large entourages ★
- Time per face / typical timeline
- Trial policy & recommended timing
- Backup-artist policy (sick/emergency) ★
- Booking/reservation fee & **payment methods (GCash, bank transfer, cash)** ★
- Deposit %, balance schedule, cancellation/rescheduling, contract
- Lead time / peak-season availability calendar ★

**Proof**
- Portfolio galleries (organized by look/skin tone; real un-retouched bridal photos, ideally in venue-like light)
- Before/after
- Reviews/ratings (verified) + testimonials
- Instagram/Facebook links; featured work / notable clients

#### 3B. Suggested taxonomy vocabulary (verified in-market)
**Categories (top level):**
- Hair & Makeup Artist (HMUA) — primary
- Bridal Makeup / Wedding Makeup Artist
- Entourage / Group Makeup
- Prenup / Pictorial Makeup
- Debut Makeup, Event/Party Makeup, Graduation/Prom Makeup, Pageant Makeup (adjacent event categories that appear on local directories like Kwyzer)
- Grooming (for men)

**Tags — occasion:** wedding, bridal, entourage, prenup/pre-nup shoot, debut (18th birthday), civil wedding, church wedding, destination wedding, birthday, corporate/event. (All verified in Philippine listings.)

**Tags — technique/finish:** airbrush makeup, traditional makeup, HD makeup, no-makeup makeup look, dewy/glowy finish, flawless, glam / full glam / soft glam, Korean look, morena-friendly, natural.

**Tags — service/logistics:** HMUA (hair + makeup), makeup only, hair only, with retouch, unli-retouch (UR), second look / change look, on-location/home service, out-of-town (OOT), team/assistants available, standby/touch-up, trial available.

**Tags — commercial:** per head / pax, bridal package, entourage package, downpayment/reservation, GCash accepted.

**Terminology confidence notes:** "HMUA," "entourage," "pax/heads," "prenup," "OOT," "unli-retouch," "morena-friendly," "airbrush/traditional/HD," "debut," "civil/church wedding," "grooming," and "Korean look/dewy/no-makeup makeup/glam" are all directly attested in Philippine MUA pages, directories, forums, and blogs. "Same Day Edit (SDE)" is a real Philippine-origin wedding term but is a **photo/video** service, not makeup — include only as an adjacent-vendor cross-reference, not an MUA tag.

#### 3C. Competitive/comparative reference — platform presentation

**Western platforms (The Knot, WeddingWire, Zola, Bridebook):**
- **Price signals:** Zola lets couples search vendor lists by price and publishes benchmark averages (combined hair+makeup ~$982; bridal makeup ~$305); The Knot/WeddingWire expose budget filters. Price transparency is a norm.
- **Filters:** category, location, budget, and style/specialty (Zola searches by signature style; The Knot/WeddingWire offer style and diversity-owned filters). WeddingWire lets couples **sort by number of reviews and average star rating**; The Knot does not sort by reviews.
- **Reviews:** central and **verified by contract/proof of payment** (WeddingWire removes reviews without a signed contract or proof of payment); award programs (Best of Weddings, Couples' Choice) reward review volume/quality. Couples are asked to leave unique reviews on each site (copy-paste hurts SEO).
- **Profiles:** photo-forward galleries, structured "Products & Services"/"Business Information," "Schedule Appointment"/"Request Quote," and storefront tiers.

**Philippine platforms:**
- **Bridestory (PH):** category "Hair & Makeup"; profile shows **$/$$/$$$ tiers (no pesos)**, star rating out of 5 with written reviews (login-gated, user-linked), portfolio "projects," and a **structured info Q&A** capturing exactly the attributes this site should filter on — "Bridal make up / Airbrush make up / Bridesmaids make up / Hairdressing / Eyelash extensions," look specialties (Natural/Romantic/Bold/Glamour), party capacity ("how many customers…if she booked her entire bridal party," 1–10 / >10), retouch sessions offered, lead time, payment systems (Cash/Bank transfer/Cheques), and a **"D-Day replacement"** question. Filters: Category + City + Budget tier + "Deals only." Instagram embed often shows "private or no photos."
- **Kasal.com:** large directory ("724 businesses" for Manila) but essentially yellow-pages — profiles show a contact block only, **"Price Upon Request" on every listing**, no portfolio, no ratings. Filters: category × city/destination.
- **eKasal.com:** category "Bridal Hair & Makeup Artists" with ~986 listings but **heavily mis-categorized** (non-beauty businesses appear), a Yelp-style **claim-listing** model, star system present but mostly "0.0 / be the first to review," no prices.
- **Kwyzer:** the most filter-rich locally — subcategories (Wedding/Freelance/Professional/Event/Debut Makeup Artist), filters for **Location + Fee/budget + availability**, list cards with **peso rate ranges ("negotiable")**, covered locations, an "Email Verified" badge, profile "Basic information" (Years of Experience, Experience With Celebrity, On Call, Works In A Salon), gallery, and FB/IG links; but **no visible star reviews** on profiles.
- **Bride and Breakfast (brideandbreakfast.ph):** editorial blog with an opt-in, browse-by-category directory ("Makeup Artists and Hairstylists"); **explicit disclaimer that listings are unverified**; no price display, no ratings, no faceted search.
- **Nuptl (nuptial-ph.com):** not a MUA directory — a couple-side planning/budget/RSVP tool + editorial cost articles (source of the tiered peso benchmarks above).

**Implication for the user's site:** the clearest wins are (1) **real price transparency** — a starting bridal rate **and** a per-head entourage rate — where local rivals hide behind "Price Upon Request"/tiers; (2) **verified reviews** (tied to a booking/inquiry) where local rivals' review systems are empty or absent; (3) **structured, filterable Philippine-specific attributes** (entourage per-head, unli-retouch, OOT coverage, early-call, airbrush/HD, morena-friendly, GCash) that Bridestory only captures as unfilterable Q&A; and (4) real portfolio galleries with skin-tone context and un-retouched bridal work.

## Recommendations

**Stage 1 — Build the MVP profile schema now.** Ship the Section 3A fields, making these **required**: solo-vs-team, starting bridal rate, per-head entourage rate, technique tags (airbrush/traditional/HD), coverage areas, trial policy, backup-artist policy, and payment methods. Make price fields structured (numeric) so they're filterable. Benchmark to change: if >30% of onboarded MUAs refuse to publish a starting rate, add a "from ₱X" minimum-only field rather than reverting to "Price Upon Request."

**Stage 2 — Ship filters that match real search behavior.** Prioritize: Location/coverage (incl. provinces/destination) → Budget (bride rate band) → Technique/finish → Entourage capacity (heads) → Availability/date. Add morena-friendly and HMUA-vs-makeup-only as toggle filters (Bridestory captures these only as static Q&A — beating that is cheap and high-value).

**Stage 3 — Make reviews trustworthy.** Gate reviews behind a booking/inquiry record (WeddingWire's proof-of-contract model) and surface review count + average rating as a **sort option** (WeddingWire's edge over The Knot). This is the single biggest trust gap in the local market.

**Stage 4 — Enforce taxonomy hygiene.** Use the controlled vocabulary in 3B; do not let free-text categories create the eKasal mis-categorization problem. Keep "SDE/prenup video" as adjacent-vendor cross-links, not MUA tags.

**Stage 5 — Add education modules that reduce the friction both sides named.** A short "what to send for an accurate quote" prompt on the inquiry form (date, venue, call time, pax, pegs, skin/allergy) directly serves MUAs' #1 ask and improves lead quality; a "how bridal pricing works (entourage/early-call/OOT)" explainer reduces the "budget?/W-word/tawad" friction.

**Metrics/thresholds that change the plan:** if inquiry-to-booking conversion lags, shorten MUA response time (add response-time badges — the fastest reply wins); if couples bounce at the profile, test showing an all-in sample quote (bride + typical entourage) rather than isolated rates.

## Caveats
- **Prices are time- and tier-sensitive.** Peso figures (bride ₱4,000–₱70,000+, entourage ₱1,500–₱5,000/head, event ₱1,500–₱2,000/head) come largely from 2015–2026 blog/directory/marketing sources and a well-structured 2026 Nuptl cost article; treat them as ranges, not fixed benchmarks, and localize by region (provincial rates run lower; Metro Manila/celebrity higher). US dollar figures are from Zola's 2026 Wedding Cost Index and The Knot's Real Weddings Study and reflect the US market only.
- **PSA seasonality nuance:** the common "December is the peak wedding month" claim is only partly right — PSA 2023 registered-marriage data puts **February first (12.7%)**, December second (10.6%), and June third (10.5%); 2024 preliminary data shows a similar February/June/December cluster. Booking-lead-time advice stands regardless.
- **Some sources are marketing.** Many "questions to ask" lists and rate pages are MUA self-promotion; the underlying consensus is reliable, but specific claims (e.g., "lashes should always be included," exact trial-timing windows) vary by artist and are opinion, not standard. The specific US cosmetology-hours figure (~1,000–1,500 supervised hours) was not independently re-verified this round and should be checked before publishing.
- **Forum/Reddit and blog evidence is anecdotal.** The "hide-the-W-word," half-face-trial, and tawad dynamics are well-attested in Philippine bridal media and quotes from named HMUAs, but reflect a spectrum of practice, not a universal rule.
- **Platform details can change.** Filter/field/review specifics for Bridestory, Kasal, eKasal, Kwyzer, The Knot, WeddingWire, and Zola were accurate as observed in mid-2026 but these products iterate; re-verify before finalizing competitive claims.
- **Nuptl naming ambiguity:** "Nuptl"/nuptial-ph.com (planning tool) is distinct from Nuptials.ph (content/directory) and Nuptials.tv — don't conflate them.
